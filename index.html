How it works

Key idea: TRU becomes useful when partners accept it for discounts. Liquidity on a DEX enables swapping, which creates a market price. Treasury can be used to support adoption (e.g., partner campaigns).

Burn mechanics (deflation)

What “burn” means Burning removes tokens from circulation by sending them to an irrecoverable state. In our approach, burn is owner-controlled and does not tax users’ transfers.
Owner-only burn
Will price increase? Burn can reduce supply, which may support price if demand stays the same or increases. Price depends on liquidity depth and market demand — no guarantees.
Market-driven price
Why avoid transfer taxes Transfer taxes often break compatibility, confuse wallets, and can harm reputation. Owner-burn is simpler and transparent.
Compatibility first
Policy suggestion: If burn is used, publish a clear rule (e.g., “Burn up to X% from treasury per quarter”) and announce each burn with a transaction link (TronScan).

Why Energy matters (your last error)

Contract deployment is a heavy operation on TRON. If you don’t have enough Energy, TRON will burn TRX as an energy fee — and if the transaction still runs out, it can fail while still costing you fees.

Best practice: stake TRX for Energy (or rent Energy) before deploying.

FAQ

Why do wallets sometimes not show new tokens automatically?

Many wallets only auto-show tokens that are known/verified or have enough activity. New contracts often require manual “Add token” until they are indexed and recognized by token lists/trackers.

What makes a token “trusted” on explorers?

Verification, complete project metadata (website, socials, logo), non-suspicious contract behavior, liquidity presence, and organic usage. Some reputation systems also rely on user reports.

Does adding more liquidity later work?

Yes. Liquidity can be increased any time by adding more assets to the pool. Removing liquidity is also possible if you hold the LP position/tokens — but removal affects price impact and market trust.
